CIRCLE

Definition

A circle is a shape with all points the same distance from its center.

General Form and Standard Equation

  • General Form: Ax² + Ay² + Dx + Ey + F = 0
  • Standard Equation: (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

Nature of Circle

All points are equidistant from the center.

Condition for the General Second Degree Equation to Represent a Circle

Coefficient of x² and y² must be equal, and there should be no xy term.

Diametric Form of Circle

Equation of a circle with diameter endpoints (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂):

(x - x₁)(x - x₂) + (y - y₁)(y - y₂) = 0

Equation of Circle Formed by Three Points

Equation of Circumscribing Circle of a Triangle Given Three Vertices

Calculate the perpendicular bisectors of the sides, and find the intersection for the center.

Intercept of the Circle on the Axes

Find where the circle intersects the x-axis and y-axis.

Position of a Point with Respect to Circle

Point (x₁, y₁): If (x₁ - h)² + (y₁ - k)² < r², it's inside; if=r², on; if> r², outside.

Power of a Point with Respect to the Circle

For point (x₁, y₁), power = (x₁ - h)² + (y₁ - k)² - r².

Least and Greatest Distance of a Point from a Circle

Distance from point (x₁, y₁) to circle center (h, k): D = √((x₁ - h)² + (y₁ - k)²). Least distance = D - r, greatest distance = D + r.

Line and a Circle

The intersection of a line and a circle can be zero, one, or two points.

Tangent Equation of a Circle

For circle (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r², tangent at point (x₁, y₁) is (x - h)(x₁ - h) + (y - k)(y₁ - k) = r².

Length of Tangents, Slope of Tangents

Length from external point to tangent: √(D² - r²). Slope of tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency.

Pair of Tangents

The combined equation of pair of tangents from a point to the circle.

Equation of Normal

The normal to the circle at point (x₁, y₁) is perpendicular to the tangent at that point.

Director Circle

The locus of the point of intersection of two perpendicular tangents to the circle.

Chord of Contacts

The chord joining the points of contact of two tangents drawn from a point outside the circle.

Chord Bisected at a Given Point

The chord that is bisected at a given point within the circle.

Common Chord, Tangent, Normal of Two Circles

The line segment joining the points of intersection of two circles is called the common chord.

Diameter of a Circle

The longest chord of the circle, passing through its center.

Angle of Intersection of Two Circles

The angle between the tangents at the points of intersection of the two circles.

Family of Circles

Circles that share a common property, such as passing through the same two points.

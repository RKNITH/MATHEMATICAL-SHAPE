Triangle

Definition

A triangle is a polygon with three edges and three vertices. It is one of the basic shapes in geometry.

Types of Triangles

Based on Sides

  • Equilateral Triangle: All sides are equal.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two sides are equal.
  • Scalene Triangle: All sides are different.

Based on Angles

  • Right Triangle: One angle is 90 degrees.
  • Acute Triangle: All angles are less than 90 degrees.
  • Obtuse Triangle: One angle is more than 90 degrees.

Formulas

Area = 0.5 * base * height

Perimeter = sum of all sides

For a right triangle: a² + b² = c² (Pythagorean Theorem)

Cosine Rule: c² = a² + b² - 2ab * cos(C)

Sine Rule: a / sin(A) = b / sin(B) = c / sin(C)=2R, where R is radius of circumcircle.

Tangent Rule (Tangent of Half-Angle): tan(A/2) = sqrt((s-b)(s-c) / s(s-a))

Length of median mₐ from vertex A: mₐ = 1/2 √(2b² + 2c² - a²)

Length of angle bisector dₐ from vertex A: dₐ = 2bc / (b+c) cos(A/2)

Length of altitude hₐ from vertex A: hₐ = 2 × Area / a

The centroid divides each median in the ratio 2:1, closer to the vertex.

The orthocenter can be inside, outside, or on the triangle depending on the type of triangle.

The incenter is equidistant from the sides of the triangle, and the distance to each side is the radius of the incircle (circle tangent to all three sides of the triangle).

In any triangle, the centroid, orthocenter, and circumcenter (center of the circumscribed circle) are collinear. The centroid divides the line joining the orthocenter and circumcenter in the ratio 2:1.

Heron's Formula

Heron's formula is used to find the area of a triangle when all three side lengths are known.

Formula: Area = √(s * (s - a) * (s - b) * (s - c))

where,

  • s = (a + b + c) / 2 (semi-perimeter of the triangle)
  • a = length of side A
  • b = length of side B
  • c = length of side C

Median

Definition: A median of a triangle is a line segment that connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.

Point of Intersection: The three medians of a triangle intersect at a single point called the centroid.

Relationships in Different Triangles:

  • Equilateral Triangle: All three medians are equal in length and intersect at the centroid.
  • Right Triangle: The median to the hypotenuse is half the length of the hypotenuse.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two medians are equal in length and intersect at the centroid.

Angle Bisector

Definition: An angle bisector of a triangle is a line segment that divides an angle into two equal parts.

Point of Intersection: The three angle bisectors of a triangle intersect at a single point called the incenter.

Relationships in Different Triangles:

  • Equilateral Triangle: All three angle bisectors are equal in length and intersect at the incenter.
  • Right Triangle: The angle bisector to the right angle divides the opposite side into segments proportional to the other two sides.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two angle bisectors are equal in length and intersect at the incenter.

Altitude

Definition: An altitude of a triangle is a perpendicular line segment from a vertex to the line containing the opposite side.

Point of Intersection: The three altitudes of a triangle intersect at a single point called the orthocenter.

Relationships in Different Triangles:

  • Equilateral Triangle: All three altitudes are equal in length and intersect at the orthocenter.
  • Right Triangle: The altitude from the right angle to the hypotenuse is equal to half the length of the hypotenuse.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two altitudes are equal in length and intersect at the orthocenter.

Circumcircle

The circumcircle of a triangle is the unique circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle.

  • It is the smallest circle that can enclose the triangle.
  • The center of the circumcircle is called the circumcenter.
  • The circumcenter is the point of concurrency of the perpendicular bisectors of the sides of the triangle.
  • The radius of the circumcircle (denoted as R) can be calculated using different formulas, such as:
    • For a triangle with sides a, b, c and area Δ:

      R = abc / (4Δ)

Incircle

The incircle of a triangle is the unique circle that is tangent to all three sides of the triangle.

Properties of the Incircle

  • The center of the incircle is called the incenter.
  • The incenter is the point of concurrency of the angle bisectors of the triangle.
  • The radius of the incircle (denoted as r) can be calculated using different formulas, such as:
    • For a triangle with sides a, b, c and semi-perimeter s:

      r = Δ / s

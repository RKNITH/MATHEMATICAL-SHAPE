Introduction to Conic Sections

Conic sections are the curves obtained by intersecting a cone with a plane. There are four types of conic sections: parabola, ellipse, hyperbola, and circle.

Geometrical Interpretation and Conic Section as a Locus of Point

A conic section can be defined as the locus of a point that moves so that its distance from a fixed point (focus) is in a constant ratio to its distance from a fixed line (directrix).

General Equation of Conic Section

The general equation of a conic section is given by:
Ax² + Bxy + Cy² + Dx + Ey + F = 0

Conditions for Different Conic Sections

  • Parabola: B² - 4AC = 0
  • Ellipse: B² - 4AC < 0
  • Hyperbola: B² - 4AC > 0
  • Circle: A = C and B = 0

General Equation of Parabola

The general equation of a parabola is:
y² = 4ax (for a horizontal parabola)
x² = 4ay (for a vertical parabola)

Details about Parabola

  • Focus: The fixed point.
  • Directrix: The fixed line.
  • Vertex: The midpoint of the focus and the directrix.
  • Latus Rectum: The line segment perpendicular to the axis of symmetry through the focus, with endpoints on the parabola.

Point and a Parabola

For a point (x, y) on a parabola, the relationship to its focus and directrix is maintained by the definition of the parabola.

Equation of Chord

The equation of a chord with endpoints (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) on the parabola y² = 4ax is given by:
t = y - mx + c where m is the slope of the chord and c is the y-intercept.

Condition for Focal Chord

A chord passing through the focus of a parabola is called a focal chord. For a parabola y² = 4ax, the condition for a focal chord is that its midpoint lies on the directrix.

Calculate Length of Focal Chord

The length of the focal chord of the parabola y² = 4ax is given by:
Length = 4a

Diameter of Parabola

For a parabola, the diameter is the line parallel to the axis of the parabola passing through the midpoint of a chord.

Tangent of Parabola

The equation of the tangent to the parabola y² = 4ax at point (x1, y1) is:
yy1 = 2a(x + x1)

Calculate Length of Tangent

The length of the tangent from a point (x1, y1) to the parabola y² = 4ax is:
Length = √(y1² - 4ax1)

Calculate Point of Intersection of Tangents

The point of intersection of tangents drawn from points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) on the parabola y² = 4ax can be found by solving the system of their tangent equations.

Director of Circle and Calculate It

The director circle of a parabola is the locus of the point of intersection of two perpendicular tangents to the parabola.

Pair of Tangents

The pair of tangents from an external point to the parabola can be found by solving the quadratic formed by substituting the point into the general equation of the parabola.

Chord of Contact

The chord of contact of tangents drawn from a point (x1, y1) to the parabola y² = 4ax is:
yy1 = 2a(x + x1)

Calculate Equation of Common Tangents of Two Parabolas

The equation of common tangents to two parabolas can be found by equating their tangent equations and solving for the common points.

Equation of Normal to Parabola

The equation of the normal to the parabola y² = 4ax at point (x1, y1) is:
y = -1/m (x - x1) + y1 where m is the slope of the tangent at (x1, y1).

Conormal Point and Calculate It

A conormal point is the point through which more than one normal passes. It can be calculated by finding the points of intersection of the normals.

Subtangent and Subnormal

For a point on a parabola y² = 4ax, the subtangent and subnormal are the projections of the tangent and normal segments on the x-axis, respectively.

  • Subtangent: The length of the projection of the tangent on the x-axis, given by 2x.
  • Subnormal: The length of the projection of the normal on the x-axis, given by 2a.

Calculate Subtangent and Subnormal

To calculate the subtangent and subnormal at a point (x1, y1) on the parabola y² = 4ax:

  • Subtangent: 2x1
  • Subnormal: 2a
